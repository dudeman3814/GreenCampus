TIPS

welcome to GreenCampus TIPS

Kitchen & cookin - Use a bowl to wash fruits/veg instead of running water Then reuse that water for plants. - Run the dishwasher only when full Surprisingly, dishwashers often use less water than hand‑washing. - Defrost food in the fridge, not under the tap Saves water and keeps food safer. 🧺 Laundry - Wash full loads Half-loads waste water and energy. - Choose quick or eco cycles Most clothes don’t need heavy-duty washing. - Reuse towels before washing Cuts laundry volume significantly. 🌿 Outdoors & plants - Water plants early morning or late evening Reduces evaporation and keeps soil moist longer. - Collect rainwater Even a small container can supply enough for houseplants or a small garden. - Choose drought‑tolerant plants They thrive with minimal watering. 🏡 Around the home - Check for hidden leaks If your water meter moves when everything is off, there’s a leak somewhere. - Insulate hot water pipes You get hot water faster, so you waste less waiting for it. 🛒 Shopping & lifestyle - Buy fewer “water-heavy” products Cotton, beef, and certain packaged goods have huge water footprints. - Choose water-efficient appliances Look for high-efficiency ratings when replacing washers or dishwashers. If you want, I can tailor these tips to your home setup—flat vs house, garden or no garden, family size, or even your cooking habits.

⚡ Energy‑Saving Tips Simple changes that noticeably cut your bills and footprint. 💡 Around the home - Switch to LED bulbs They use far less energy and last much longer. - Turn off lights when leaving a room Sounds basic, but it genuinely adds up. - Unplug idle devices Chargers, TVs, consoles, and microwaves draw “phantom power” even when off. 🧺 Appliances & daily use - Use eco or low‑temperature wash cycles Most clothes clean perfectly at cooler settings. - Air‑dry clothes when possible Dryers are among the biggest energy consumers. - Run appliances only when full Dishwashers and washing machines are most efficient at full loads. 🌡️ Heating & cooling - Lower the thermostat slightly Even a small reduction can save a surprising amount of energy. - Use curtains smartly Open them for natural warmth; close them at night to keep heat in. - Seal drafts Gaps around doors and windows leak heat and waste energy. ♻️ Recycling Guidance Recycling works best when you follow a few simple rules. 🗂️ Know what your local council accepts Recycling rules vary by area. Check what Burton upon Trent’s local collection allows so you’re not “wish‑cycling” (putting in items that contaminate the batch). 🧴 Clean & dry items - Rinse bottles, cans, and containers - Let them dry before tossing them in Moisture can ruin paper and cardboard in the same bin. 📦 Flatten cardboard Saves space and makes collection more efficient. 🥤 Separate materials If an item has mixed materials (like a cardboard sleeve on a plastic cup), separate them before recycling. 🚫 Common items that look recyclable but aren’t - Greasy pizza boxes - Plastic film and cling wrap - Coffee cups with plastic lining - Foil‑lined packets (crisps, some chocolates) ✔️ Items that are often recyclable but overlooked - Aerosol cans (empty) - Shampoo/soap bottles - Tin foil (clean and scrunched into a ball) - Glass jars and bottles (lids off) 🌍 Bonus: Reduce before you recycle Recycling is good, but reducing waste is even better. - Choose products with minimal packaging - Buy refillable or bulk items - Reuse jars, bags, and containers - Repair items instead of replacing them
